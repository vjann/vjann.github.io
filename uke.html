It's definitely not flushed out but here's the basics: You can use the keyboard as the frets on the ukelele-- for example, to strum a G chord you would hold down '2', 'e', and 'd' keys and pass over all the strings with your mouse. To 'learn' or get help on chords, The buttons ('G', 'C', 'Am') are pressible and it'll give you a guide at the bottom. Another way to strum is to use the 'UP' and 'DOWN' arrows on the keyboard.

  • Strumming: Pass mouse over the strings or use 'UP' and 'DOWN' arrows
  • Chords/Fretting: Follow the diagram on the bottom left. For example, holding down 'X' would give you an Am chord.
  • Pressing the Chord Buttons will show you the chord fingerings!

There are lots of limitations/future improvements:

  • The UI isn't great, especially needing an introduction screen, instructions, etc.
  • I can always add more chords and notes
  • Something easy I could add is dynamic volume, so softer with slower strums and louder with faster strums
  • A cool idea I'd like to add one day is feedback on what keys the user is currently pressing, either on the uke or on the chord helper at the bottom
