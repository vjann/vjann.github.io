HDPhoto Mobile

My largest solo project to date.

An app to support my uncle's small print shop. I had nothing better to spend my winter break, and I got to visit relatives and explore Taipei!

TL;DR

If you just want to see the finished product, here's the beta play store link (android only for now): Play Store. It's an app that lets you pick/upload photos, pay, and wait two weeks for a photobook to show up on your doormat.

The Product

Simply put, the user-flow is create album, add pictures, upload, enter customer/shipping info, pay.

Image Picking (and Album Creation)

There are two main ways of selecting photos: manual selection through your camera roll (left) or by sharing a Google Photos Album into the app! The upload process can be slow, depending on your connection, but at least it runs in the background, so you can keep choosing photos in the meantime. Don't worry, I made sure to ask the user for permission before using their precious mobile data.

Updating the album

Under the my uncle's mantra "Keep it Simple", I sought to strike a balance between functionality and friendliness. My approach: the 4 button UI (and an editable album name denoted with a pencil logo). Hopefully, the sole reason it's not self explanatory is because it's in Chinese. The four main functions translated are pick images, delete album, upload/submit, and instructions.

Boring Stuff

And then there's just the fluff needed for the product. That includes product pitch, FAQ, order receipts, and payment. A cool thing about the receipts is that it's updated with your order status (in orange text), so you know exactly where in the production process your photobook is!

The Process

Punished by a Plurality of Pitfalls

Challenges and Growth

This was the first time I've used React Native/Javascript or Firebase. It was much bigger than any other software project I've done, and I was the sole developer. That meant that as hard as I tried, I experienced the difficulty of code design and architecture, especially one that minimizes technical debt. For example, I realized much later that the albums on an Android were figments of Google Photo's software-- you couldn't directly share a photo folder into the app because it didn't exist in the device (only in Google Photos)! Plus, what if you saved space by backing up and deleting your photos from your device?

Due to my lack of initial experience, I found myself rewriting code almost as much as I was writing it. Through this process, I've gained a better grasp on software architecture, UI, API's, and platform specific development.
